Research

Our group studies secure, scalable, and trustworthy distributed systems. The themes below describe current and planned directions for the lab. They can be updated as projects and publications develop.

Blockchain and Payment Channel Networks

We study blockchain systems, distributed ledgers, and payment channel networks, with particular interest in the Bitcoin Lightning Network. Topics include routing, scalability, security, privacy, and measurement of deployed decentralized systems.

Peer-to-Peer Networks and Network Security

We investigate how distributed systems communicate and defend themselves under realistic and adversarial network conditions. This includes P2P overlays, secure routing, resilient network design, and trustworthy decentralized infrastructure.

Applied Cryptography

We are interested in practical cryptographic protocols for real systems, including Multi-Party Computation, Zero-Knowledge Proofs, privacy-preserving computation, and secure protocol design.

Post-Quantum and Quantum-Resilient Security

We study security mechanisms that prepare distributed systems and blockchain infrastructure for the post-quantum era. Current interests include post-quantum cryptography, cryptographic agility, hybrid protocols, and deployment tradeoffs.
