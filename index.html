BOLT (Blockchain, Optimized Network, Ledger, Trust)

The BOLT Research Group focuses on designing secure, scalable, and trustworthy distributed systems at the intersection of cybersecurity, blockchain technology, computer networks, and applied cryptography. Our research spans cryptographic protocols, peer to peer (P2P) networks, network security, distributed ledgers, and trustworthy computing, with an emphasis on bridging theoretical advances and practical system design.

The name BOLT also reflects our recent work on the Bitcoin Lightning Network, emphasizing fast, scalable, and secure payment channel networks. More recently, the group has expanded its research into advanced cryptographic techniques, including Multi Party Computation (MPC), Zero Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs), and Post Quantum Cryptography (PQC), which are foundational technologies for the next generation of secure and privacy preserving Web3 systems. We are also interested in developing quantum resilient security mechanisms to prepare distributed systems and blockchain infrastructures for the post quantum era.

By combining foundational networking technologies with modern cryptographic techniques, the BOLT Research Group aims to advance both the theory and practice of secure, scalable, and trustworthy distributed systems.

Research Areas

  • Blockchain and distributed systems
  • Lightning Network and payment channels
  • P2P networks and network security
  • Applied and post-quantum cryptography
